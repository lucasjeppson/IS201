How to Ski: A Beginner's Guide

Learning to ski can be an exciting winter adventure. Below are the essential steps to get started, along with helpful links for deeper learning.

  • Start by understanding the basics of skiing and the equipment involved.
  • Wear proper winter clothing and layers to stay warm and dry.
  • Learn how to put on your ski boots and attach them to your skis.
  • Begin on a gentle slope and practice the snowplow or "pizza" stance for control.
  • Take a lesson from a certified instructor for proper form and safety.
  • Practice getting up after a fallâ€”it's part of the learning process!
  • Learn how to use a ski lift safely and correctly (ski lift).
  • Gradually increase speed and difficulty as your confidence grows.
  • Review basic skiing etiquette and slope rules (Responsibility Code).
  • Stay hydrated, take breaks, and enjoy the mountain environment.

Utah Ski Resorts

Park City Mountain

Pros: Largest ski resort in the U.S., lots of terrain variety, great for beginners and intermediates, excellent town amenities.

Cons: Can get very crowded, higher prices, parking challenges.

Typical Weather: Cold, consistent snowfall, with many sunny days. Storms bring dry Utah powder.

Deer Valley Resort

Pros: Excellent grooming, luxury experience, fantastic beginner-friendly terrain, no snowboarding allowed (pro or con depending on preference).

Cons: More expensive, limited steep terrain for advanced skiers.

Typical Weather: Steady snowfall and cool temperatures that keep groomed runs in great shape.

Snowbird

Pros: Some of the best powder in Utah, long season, advanced terrain, and stunning scenery.

Cons: Not ideal for beginners; can be steep and intimidating.

Typical Weather: Heavy snowfall, colder temps, and frequent storms that create deep powder days.

Alta Ski Area

Pros: Legendary powder, skier-only mountain, classic alpine feel, excellent terrain for intermediate and advanced skiers.

Cons: No snowboarding, limited nightlife, not as beginner-focused.

Typical Weather: Very snowy with extremely light, dry powder due to altitude and climate.

Solitude Mountain Resort

Pros: Less crowded, great snow, strong beginner terrain, good value.

Cons: Smaller resort compared to others, quieter village scene.

Typical Weather: Cold, snowy winters with a reputation for excellent powder days.

Brighton Resort

Pros: Affordable, very beginner-friendly, excellent snowboarding terrain, strong night skiing program.

Cons: Fewer amenities and lodging options.

Typical Weather: High snowfall totals and cooler temps that keep snow conditions excellent.
